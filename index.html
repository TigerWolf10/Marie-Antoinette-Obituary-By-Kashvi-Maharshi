Marie Antoinette 1755-1793


Marie Antoinette, despised queen of France, has died via execution by guillotine on October 16, 1793 - aged 37. She was beheaded in the Place de la Révolution.

Life

Marie Antoinette was born on 2 November 1755. She was born an Archduchess of Austria, and was the youngest daughter of Empress Maria Theresa and Emperor Francis I. She married Louis XVI when she was 14, in 1770.She lived at the Hofburg Palace in Vienna, but also spent much of her childhood in Schönbrunn, their summer home in Vienna. She was not an academic child, being unable to write correctly in German or any other language commonly used in court at the time, however she was a good musician; she learned to play harp, harpsichord and flute. She was also a talented dancer, and was very fond of dolls.


People's opinion on her

Marie Antoinette, soon after her marrige to the king, became increasingly unpopular due to her reputation of living an excessively lavish and wasteful lifestyle, gambling and rumours of having many affairs and illegitimate children. She was also suspected of harboring sympathies for enemies of Frane - particularly her homeland of Austria. She was ignorant and frivolous with money, whilst most of the general public suffered due to starvation and poverty. But despite all this, she was already distrusted by the French people due to her Austrian heritage, and this was only further fueled by all the rumours and her extravagant lifestyle. The intense despising may not entirely be down to only her personality, but may be in fact partially attributed to the bias that she was indisposed to; her Austrian heritage.
